Research

We partner with schools, families, and communities to promote physical activity, reduce sedentary time, support healthy eating, and strengthen sleep routines in children — with a focus on equity and real-world impact.

Ongoing

ALL-PLAY: Parent–Child Exergaming and Physical Activity

Design: Lab-based experiment • Setting: Movement lab • Population: Preschoolers (3–5 years) and parents

We test whether preschoolers are more physically active when they play active video games alone versus when they play together with a parent. Activity levels are measured under both conditions to understand how parent involvement influences movement.

SUNRISE: 24-Hour Movement Behaviors in Early Childhood

Design: Surveillance / Observational • Setting: Preschool & community sites • Population: 3–5 years

U.S. arm of a global study benchmarking how young children move, sleep, and sit. Measures physical activity, sedentary time, and sleep against international 24-hour movement guidelines, along with diet and health indicators.

SCREEN-SMART: Family Coaching to Reduce Sedentary Time

Design: Pragmatic RCT • Setting: Home & Head Start • Population: Families with preschoolers

Family-centered coaching and media plans to shift screen habits, increase light activity, and support healthier snacking routines.

SLEEP-WELL: Teacher-Delivered Routines for Better Sleep

Design: Hybrid I • Setting: Preschool classrooms • Population: 3–5 years

Brief, teacher-delivered lessons and parent nudges to align daytime activity with consistent evening routines and earlier bedtimes.
