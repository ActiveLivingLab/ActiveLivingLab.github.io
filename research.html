Research

How can we create conditions that enable everyone—regardless of background or circumstance—to live active, healthy, and meaningful lives? Across communities, profound inequities in access to safe spaces, resources, and opportunities for movement continue to shape health outcomes. Yet, like scientific tools that once transformed human understanding, physical activity and health promotion can be seen as powerful social and behavioral tools—mechanisms that empower individuals and communities to flourish. At the Active Living Lab (ALL), we advance health equity through evidence-based strategies that promote physical activity and health. Our research examines how environments, programs, and policies can be designed to make healthy choices accessible, sustainable, and culturally relevant for all. Grounded in behavior change theory and guided by community engagement, we collaborate with schools, families, and local organizations to co-create solutions that bridge research and practice. Through this work, we aim not only to understand the determinants of active living but also to transform them—helping communities move toward healthier, more equitable futures.
