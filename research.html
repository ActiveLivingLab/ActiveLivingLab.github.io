Research

How can we create conditions that enable everyone—regardless of background or circumstance—to live active, healthy, and meaningful lives? Across communities, profound inequities in access to safe spaces, resources, and opportunities for movement continue to shape health outcomes. Yet, like scientific tools that once transformed human understanding, physical activity and health promotion can be seen as powerful social and behavioral tools—mechanisms that empower individuals and communities to flourish.

At the Active Living Lab (ALL), we advance health equity through evidence-based strategies that promote physical activity and health. Our research examines how environments, programs, and policies can be designed to make healthy choices accessible, sustainable, and culturally relevant for all.

Grounded in behavior change theory and guided by community engagement, we collaborate with schools, families, and local organizations to co-create solutions that bridge research and practice. Through this work, we aim not only to understand the determinants of active living but also to transform them—helping communities move toward healthier, more equitable futures. To learn more about our research, visit our Projects and Publications pages.

Ongoing

ALL-PLAY: Parent–Child Exergaming and Physical Activity

Design: Lab-based experiment • Setting: Movement lab • Population: Preschoolers (3–5 years) and parents

We test whether preschoolers are more physically active when they play active video games alone versus when they play together with a parent. Activity levels are measured under both conditions to understand how parent involvement influences movement.

Ongoing

SUNRISE: 24-Hour Movement Behaviors in Early Childhood

Design: Surveillance / Observational • Setting: Preschool & community sites • Population: 3–5 years

U.S. arm of a global study benchmarking how young children move, sleep, and sit. Measures physical activity, sedentary time, and sleep against international 24-hour movement guidelines, along with diet and health indicators.

Development

SCREEN-SMART: Family Coaching to Reduce Sedentary Time

Design: Pragmatic RCT • Setting: Home & Head Start • Population: Families with preschoolers

Family-centered coaching and media plans to shift screen habits, increase light activity, and support healthier snacking routines.

Development

SLEEP-WELL: Teacher-Delivered Routines for Better Sleep

Design: Hybrid I • Setting: Preschool classrooms • Population: 3–5 years

Brief, teacher-delivered lessons and parent nudges to align daytime activity with consistent evening routines and earlier bedtimes.

Planning

HEART: Healthy Eating & Active Recreation for Tribes

Design: Community-based participatory research • Setting: Tribal communities

Co-creating culturally grounded PA and nutrition strategies with community leaders to support sustainable behavior change.

Methods

Better Trials: Practical Randomization & Reporting for School Settings

Design: Methods & guidance • Setting: Schools & childcare

Templates and reporting guides (CONSORT-aligned) to make rigorous trials feasible in real-world education environments.
